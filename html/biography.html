EARLY LIFE

Bhaktivedanta Srila Prahbupada was born Abbay Charan De on September 1, 1896 to a pious Hindu family in Calcutta (now Kolkata) India. He was also called Nandulal. His father was Gour Mohan De, who worked as a cloth merchant, and his mother was Rajani De. Consistent with Bengali traditions, Abbay charan’s parents invited an astrologer to predict his horoscope. The astrologer subsequently professed to De’s family that later in his life, Abbay would become a” great exponent of religion.” Abbay, however, grew up in a Vaishanav home (pious Hindu family) and his father was an ardent devotee in his spiritual faith and practices. Gour Mohan on regular basis took Abbay to the Radha- Govinda temple as a child. Later in life, Srila Prahbupada fondly remembers the murtis (architectural and paraphernalia outfit) of that temple. During his first year, his family arranged for his marriage with Radharani Datta. Until completing his fourth year in the College, Abbay lived with his family and Radharani lived with hers. In 1920, Abbay chose not to accept his graduation diploma, and as a youth growing up in the British controlled India, he became involved with Mahatma Gandhi’s civil disobedience movement to secure independent for his nation. -And while working at a small pharmaceutical business in 1922, he met and became a disciple of a prominent scholar and spiritual master; Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati (1874-1937), which significantly proved most influential on Abbay’s future life, ordination and calling. Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati, was a leader in the Gaudiya Vaishnava denomination, a monotheistic tradition within the broad Hindu culture, and asked Abbay Charan Aravinda Nandulal to bring the teachings of Lord Sri Krishna to the English –speaking world after his initiation into the faith from the same in Allahabad, in 1933; where he finally resolved to carry out his mentor’s instruction which later awarded him the honorific names as; A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prahbupada, who later spent the next 32 years preparing for the great missionary journey to the western.

SPIRITUALITY

In 1944, A. C. Bhaktivedanta Srila prahbupada started the publication called ‘Back to Godhead Magazine’ for which he was the writer, designer, publisher, editor and distributor all together. His spiritual master, Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati, instructed him to spread the message of the supreme Lord Sri Caitanya Mhaprahbu (Gods incarnate) in the English language for the English speaking nations of the world. In 1947, the Gaudiya Vaishnava Society gave him the title Bhaktivedanta (Bhakti-vedanta). In the 1960s, after founding the International Society for Krishna Consciousness ISKCON became known by the honorific Prahbupada. From 1950 onwards, he lived at the medieval Radha-Damodar mandir in the holy town of Vridavan, where he began his commentary and translation work of the Sanskrit work –the Bhagavata Purana. His Guru, Bhaktisiddhanta Sarasvati, had always encouraged him to publish books, referring to the need for the literary presentation of the Vaishnava culture.

MISSION TO THE WESTERN WORLD

In 1965, at the age of sixty –nine, Srila Prahbupada travelled to New York City aboard a cargo ship. The journey was treacherous, and the elderly spiritual teacher suffered two heart attacks aboard ship. Arriving in the United Sates with just seven US dollars in Indian rupees and his translations of sacred Sanskrit texts, Srila Prahbupada began to share the timeless wisdom of Krishna consciousness. His message of peace and goodwill resonated with many young people, some of whom came forward to become serious students of the Krishna tradition. With the help of these students, Srila Prahbupada rented a small storefront on New York’s Lower East Side to use as a temple. On July 11, 1966, he officially registered his organization in the state of New York, formally founding the International Society of Krishna Consciousness. In the eleven years that followed, Srila Prahbupada circled the entire globe fourteen times on lecture tours, bringing the teachings of Lord Sri Krishna to thousands of people on six continents. Men and women from all backgrounds and walks of life came forward to accept his divine message, and with their help, Srila Prahbupada established ISKCON centers and projects throughout the world – the same and exact mission and spiritual objective of this present movement, in the form and style of a commissioned global peace initiatives and charity organization. Under his inspiration as same as our proposals represents, Krishna devotees established temples, rural communities, educational institutions, and started what would become the world’s largest vegetarian food relief programs, etc. With the desire to nourish the roots of Krishna Consciousness in its home, Srila Prahbupada returned to India several times, where he sparked a revival in the Vaishnava tradition. In India, he opened dozens of temples, including large centers in the holy towns of Vrindavan and Mayapur.

GREATEST ACHIEVEMENTS

Srila Prahbupada significant contributions, perhaps, are his many books. He authored over seventy volumes on the Krishna tradition, which are highly respected by scholars for their authority, depth, fidelity to the tradition, and clarity. Several of his works are being used as textbooks in numerous college courses. His writings have been translated into 76 languages. His most prominent works includes in lists; The Bhagavad –gita As It Is, the thirty Volume Srimad –Bhagavatam, And the 17- volume Sri Caitanya Caritamrita, to mention but very few.
